The Tom Brown Principle

"It's the quality of the argument, not the power of the words, that converts."

Argument Structure

  1. Acknowledge current belief
  2. Show why it's incomplete
  3. Introduce necessary belief with evidence
  4. Make new belief undeniable
  5. Present solution aligned with belief

Before/After Framework

BEFORE (7 elements): Failed product, financial pain, visual frustration, behavioral evidence, emotional cost, relationship impact, identity statement

AFTER (7 transformations): Visual change, sensory win, behavioral change, social liberation, relationship healing, photo freedom, identity reclaim

What Gets Approved ‚úÖ

Before/after EXPERIENCE ‚Ä¢ Financial pain ‚Ä¢ Emotional acknowledgment ‚Ä¢ Non-medical conspiracy ‚Ä¢ General social proof

What Gets Rejected ‚ùå

Medical claims ‚Ä¢ Percentage claims ‚Ä¢ B/A body photos ‚Ä¢ Absolute removal ‚Ä¢ Medical procedure comparisons
