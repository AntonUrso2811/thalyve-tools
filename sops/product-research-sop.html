1. The Winning Product Philosophy

Making money in eCommerce comes down to one thing: providing value to the market. Your income is a direct reflection of how much value you deliver. In eCommerce, value is delivered through problem solving.

Core Principle
Do not focus on "making money." Focus on solving real problems. Money is the byproduct.

1.1 Problem Solving ‚Äî The Foundation

Every winning product solves a problem. That problem can be:

  • Physical ‚Äî pain, discomfort, inconvenience
  • Emotional ‚Äî confidence, self-expression, identity
  • Convenience-based ‚Äî time, effort, frustration
  • Identity-based ‚Äî fashion, status, belonging
Key Insight
Problem-solving is not binary (yes/no). It's a scale. The more painful the problem, the higher the likelihood of impulse purchases, fast decisions, and emotional buying.

Some niches naturally contain stronger problems. However, problem-heavy niches often come with higher ad costs, more competition, and stricter compliance.

1.3 Economics ‚Äî If the Numbers Don't Work, Nothing Works

The two main costs in eCommerce:

  1. Product cost (COGs + shipping)
  2. Advertising cost (CPA)

If your product can't cover both and leave profit ‚Äî it's not viable.

Non-Negotiable Baseline
  • Average CPA ‚âà $20
  • Minimum selling price target ‚âà $40
  • You need at least one: 3√ó markup OR $40+ front-end price

1.4 Proof of Demand ‚Äî You Cannot Create Demand

One of the biggest mistakes is trying to invent demand. You can't.

Correct Question
‚ùå "Is this product unique?"
‚úÖ "Are people already buying this?"

The truth about saturation: Global eCommerce = $6+ trillion/year. Even niches worth $50 billion annually are nowhere near saturated. If someone is doing $1M/month, they're still less than 1% of that category. Competition is a green light, not a red flag.

Your job is to do it better or do it different:

  • Target a different market (US ‚Üí AU, EU, etc.)
  • Change the angle (who it's for + why)
  • Improve the offer (pricing, bundles, bonuses)
  • Find a newer/better version of the product

1.5 Sufficient Content ‚Äî The Silent Killer

A product can check every box and still fail if you can't market it. Modern eCommerce is visual. No content = no leverage.

Hard Rule
If you cannot reasonably get enough content (images, videos, angles) ‚Äî it is not a winning product.

Options if content is limited:

  • Order the product yourself
  • Have a friend or family member use it
  • Take your own photos/videos
  • Work with creators
  • Use a product already proven with content available

3. The Research Method ‚Äî Step by Step

1 Setup GetHookd
  • Create a folder called "Product Research"
  • Add a Board inside it (use the Default board ‚Äî centralise everything)
  • Open alongside: Mutual Dropshipping, FB Ad Library, AliExpress, SimilarWeb, Amazon
2 Apply Filters

Use ONE of the following mandatory filters:

FilterWhat It Means
WinningAd running 7+ days + backend algorithm signals = likely profitable
Runtime > 7 / 14 / 21 daysStill spending money on FB = most likely making money

Optional filters: ad type (images only works well), niche (not perfect ‚Äî uses brand name/headline, do own due diligence).

Pro tip: Close the left filter panel to see 6 ads instead of 5. If one brand dominates, use the three dots ‚Üí "Hide brand" to keep discovery fresh.

3 Click Through to Store
  • Click "Shop Now" on any promising ad
  • Land on the product/website page
  • Begin validation process (Steps 4-6)
4 Traffic Validation ‚Äî SimilarWeb

Quick check: Click the SimilarWeb extension on the competitor's site.

Minimum Threshold
Target: 30,000+ visitors (check relevance to recent timeframes)

Deep check: Search the store on SimilarWeb website for full details ‚Äî monthly traffic, similar competitors, market behaviour. Note: shows previous month only, not current.

5 Ad Volume Validation ‚Äî Facebook Ad Library

From GetHookd: click the ad ‚Üí open source link ‚Üí go to FB Ad Library.

Minimum Threshold
Filter ‚Üí Active ads only ‚Üí Need at least 10 active ads

Key actions:

  • Sort by longest-running ads (oldest = most proven)
  • If brand sells multiple products: use Ctrl+Cmd+F to search for specific product terms
  • Ensure you understand where the traffic is actually going
  • Note: GetHookd saves ads forever ‚Äî FB Ad Library does not
6 Product Sourcing ‚Äî Mutual Dropshipping
  1. Save the product image from the competitor site (or use Image Downloader extension ‚Äî some brands block right-click)
  2. Search on AliExpress ‚Äî use "Search by Image" next to the search icon, upload the saved image
  3. Find the core product ‚Äî pick listings with most sales. If bundles appear, click through to get the main product page
  4. Copy the AliExpress link (avoid bundle listing links)
  5. Submit to Mutual Dropshipping:
    • Go to Sourcing ‚Üí Individual Products (top right: Sourcing Requests)
    • Upload: product name + images
    • Untick "Similar Products"
    • Product type: Dropshipping
    • Mutual sources from their own factories and creates a product listing
  6. Get your true cost: COGs + Shipping Fee (adjust shipping destination for most accurate quote, pick shipping method that makes most sense)
  7. Replace the AliExpress link with the Mutual link once generated
7 Set Pricing
  • Match competitor pricing initially ‚Äî keep it simple
  • Goal: understand margins first, get creative later
  • Can offer something enticing, but simplicity wins early on
8 Log Everything in the Template

Fill in every column of the Product Research Template (see Section 5). This feeds directly into review and approval.

9 Content Validation
  • Check competitor ads for content quality and volume
  • Check Amazon listings ‚Äî typically better assets than AliExpress
  • Use Google Lens on Amazon to find additional listings (snowball effect)
  • Assess: enough videos, images, and angles to create store + ads?
Remember
‚ùå Do NOT use Amazon for pricing ‚Äî IGNORE Amazon prices
‚úÖ Use Amazon for content inspiration only

6. Economics & B/E ROAS

What Is B/E ROAS?

Break-Even Return on Ad Spend ‚Äî the minimum ROAS needed to not lose money.

B/E ROAS = Selling Price √∑ (Selling Price ‚àí Total Cost)

Spreadsheet formula: =IFERROR(K/(K‚àíF),"")

Example: Cost = $25, Selling Price = $75 ‚Üí B/E ROAS = 75 √∑ 50 = 1.5

Meaning: For every $1 you spend on ads, you need $1.50 back just to break even.

B/E ROAS Thresholds

B/E ROASSignalMeaning
Below 1.5Strong candidateGood margins, room to scale, absorb ad cost fluctuations
‚âà 1.5Acceptable‚âà 3√ó markup ‚Äî the standard benchmark
Above 1.5High riskThin margins, hard to profit, little room for error
Rule of Thumb
B/E ROAS ‚â§ 1.5 = 3√ó markup = ideal zone. Lower is better. The higher you can price (while it makes sense), the more favourable your economics.

Pricing Rules

Price RangeVerdictNotes
$40‚Äì$100‚úÖ Sweet spotSupports paid ads, allows impulse buying, balances conversion + margin
~$30‚ö†Ô∏è Can workHarder to scale, usually outside US, not ideal for health/beauty, often requires worldwide markets
$100+‚ö†Ô∏è AdvancedLonger consideration cycle, fewer impulse buys, requires branding + email/SMS + retargeting, slower testing

The MRR/Upsell Exception

You can break the $40 rule if:

  • The product can be purchased multiple times (repeat purchases)
  • Strong upsells or bundles increase AOV
  • This allows lower front-end pricing with higher lifetime value

A product that gets bought 2, 3, 4 times ‚Äî that's a true winning product.

Product Size & Quality

  • Heavy/bulky = higher shipping = lower margins ‚Üí focus on lightweight, compact
  • Poor quality = high refund rates = destroyed margins ‚Üí check reviews, order samples
  • Partial refund strategy: Customer keeps the product (return shipping from US to China is expensive). You lose some money but avoid full loss.

8. Winning Product Checklist

Use this before logging any product. All boxes must be checked.

Pre-Log Checklist

  • Problem: Solves a real, identifiable problem (physical, emotional, convenience, or identity)
  • Niche: Sits in a viable niche appropriate for experience level
  • Economics: 3√ó markup achievable OR $40+ front-end price
  • Demand: People are already buying this (competitor evidence exists)
  • Traffic: SimilarWeb shows 30,000+ visitors on competitor store
  • Ads: Competitor has 10+ active ads in FB Ad Library
  • Runtime: Ads running 7+ days (spending money = likely profitable)
  • Content: Sufficient images, videos, and angles available to create store + ads
  • Size/Weight: Lightweight, compact (no margin-killing shipping costs)
  • Quality: Reviews checked, no major quality/refund red flags
  • Sourced: Submitted to Mutual Dropshipping with accurate COGs + shipping

9. Daily Research Workflow

Morning Research Block (30‚Äì60 mins)
  1. Open GetHookd ‚Üí apply Winning or Runtime filter
  2. Scroll through ads ‚Äî save anything promising to your Product Research board
  3. For each saved product, run the validation gates:
    • SimilarWeb ‚Üí 30K+ traffic?
    • FB Ad Library ‚Üí 10+ active ads?
    • Economics ‚Üí can you get 3√ó markup?
    • Content ‚Üí enough to build with?
  4. Products that pass all gates ‚Üí log in the template
  5. Products that fail ‚Üí skip, move on, no attachment
Sourcing Block (as needed)
  1. For logged products: find on AliExpress (image search)
  2. Submit to Mutual Dropshipping
  3. Once Mutual responds: update Total Cost + replace link
  4. Calculate B/E ROAS ‚Üí still green/yellow? Continue. Red? Drop it.
Weekly Review
  1. Review all logged products
  2. Narrow to top 10
  3. Submit for review/approval
  4. Get 1 product approved ‚Üí move to testing phase

10. Alternative Discovery Methods

Snowball Method

  • Take a keyword you already found from a winning product
  • Search variations in GetHookd
  • Explore adjacent products
  • Follow intuition ‚Äî but always verify with data

FB Ads Library ‚Üí GetHookd

  • Search directly in FB Ad Library for product categories
  • Use the GetHookd browser extension to save ads you find
  • Saves directly to your Product Research board
Remember
Be creative with discovery, but disciplined with validation. Every product still needs to pass all five gates regardless of how you found it.

11. Non-Negotiable Rules

The Rules
  1. Every column gets filled. No half-logged products. If you can't fill it, it's not ready.
  2. B/E ROAS ‚â§ 1.5 or drop it. Don't fall in love with products that can't make money.
  3. 30K+ SimilarWeb + 10+ Active Ads. Below this = insufficient proof of demand.
  4. No sourcing from AliExpress. AliExpress is reference only. Mutual Dropshipping is the supplier.
  5. No product moves forward without approval. Log ‚Üí narrow to 10 ‚Üí review ‚Üí 1 approved ‚Üí go.
  6. Content is non-negotiable. No visuals = no marketing = no sales.
  7. You are not rewarded for creativity in research. You are rewarded for discipline and consistency.
